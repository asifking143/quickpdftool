About Color Palette Types

Analogous

Colors that are next to each other on the color wheel. Creates harmonious and pleasing designs.

Complementary

Colors opposite each other on the color wheel. Creates high contrast and vibrant looks.

Triadic

Three colors evenly spaced around the color wheel. Offers strong visual contrast while retaining harmony.

Tetradic

Four colors arranged into two complementary pairs. Offers rich color variety.

Monochromatic

Variations in lightness and saturation of a single color. Creates cohesive and elegant designs.

Split-Complementary

A base color plus the two colors adjacent to its complement. High contrast but less tension than complementary.
