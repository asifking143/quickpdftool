About QR Codes & Error Correction

What is a QR Code?

A QR code (Quick Response code) is a type of matrix barcode that can store various types of data, including URLs, text, contact information, and more. QR codes are designed to be quickly scanned by smartphones and other devices.

Error Correction

QR codes include error correction to ensure they can still be read even if partially damaged. Higher error correction levels make QR codes more robust but also increase their size.

Low (L) 7%
Medium (M) 15%
Quartile (Q) 25%
High (H) 30%

Best Practices

• For printed materials, use higher error correction (Q or H)

• For digital use, Medium (M) is usually sufficient

• Test your QR code with multiple devices before distribution

• Keep QR code content concise for better readability
